Clean Water

Agricultural watersheds in the Midwestern US underwent extensive change when increased availability of synthetic fertilizers became widely available in the early 1900s. These changes have had enormous positive impacts on the agricultural economy and the expansion of agricultural exports. However, intensive cropland activity contributes to soil quality degradation and excess runoff of nutrients from agriculture fields that result in water quality problems including contaminated drinking water, algal blooms, and associated periods of little to no oxygen in downstream waters.
